Why Having a Primary Care Provider (PCP) Matters

Your PCP Benefits

A primary care provider is your healthcare home base. Having a consistent PCP leads to better health outcomes and lower healthcare costs.

How your PCP strengthens your health:

  • Builds your complete health history to spot patterns and potential issues
  • Coordinates with specialists to ensure comprehensive care
  • Recommends the right screenings based on your personal risk factors
  • Provides personalized health guidance for your unique needs

Prostate Health

Prostate cancer affects 1 in 8 men during their lifetime. Early detection with regular screenings dramatically improves treatment success.

Your next appointment checklist:

  • Ask about PSA test and Digital Rectal Exam scheduling (both covered as preventive care)
  • Discuss your personal risk factors including family history and lifestyle
  • Review screening options and create a personalized schedule
  • Plan for regular follow-up care and monitoring

Focus on Prostate Health

Prostate cancer is one of the most common cancers in men. Early detection through regular screenings can make a significant difference. Talk to your doctor about:

  • When to start prostate cancer screenings
  • Your personal risk factors
  • Available screening options
