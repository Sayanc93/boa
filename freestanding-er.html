Did you know? Freestanding ERs can cost up to 10x more than urgent care for the same treatment.

Many Aetna members visit freestanding emergency rooms for non-life-threatening conditions, resulting in unexpectedly high bills. These facilities often look like urgent care centers but charge hospital emergency room prices.

What is a freestanding ER?

A standalone emergency facility not physically connected to a hospital that charges the same rates as hospital ERs but may not offer the same level of services or specialists.

When to Use the Emergency Room

True emergencies that require an ER visit:

  • Chest pain or difficulty breathing
  • Severe bleeding that can't be stopped
  • Severe burns or wounds
  • Suspected stroke (sudden numbness, confusion)
  • Severe head or neck injuries

If you're experiencing a life-threatening emergency, call 911 or go to the nearest hospital emergency room immediately.
